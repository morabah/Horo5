## **1.1  Startup Launch Phase Strategy**

NEW IN V1.9 — Wirtz §17.5 specifies the priority sequence for a new online brand with no heritage, no reviews, and no customer history. HORO is at the beginning of this sequence and must not skip stages.

| Stage | What it means for HORO | Mechanism | Priority |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1\. Create brand interest | First-time visitors have no reason to trust HORO yet. The page must generate interest before asking for a purchase. | Art-hero layout. Illustration is the first thing seen. Strong visual identity. Meaning-first copy. | **LAUNCH CRITICAL** |
| 2\. Encourage first test purchase | The first buyer takes the biggest risk. Remove every friction point between interest and order. | COD (zero payment risk). WhatsApp ordering. Process proof replaces missing reviews. Scarcity signals (quantity, not time). | **LAUNCH CRITICAL** |
| 3\. Motivate repeat purchases | Only possible after stage 2 has a sufficient sample. Requires order history data. | Post-purchase WhatsApp follow-up. Artist archive content. New drop announcement to previous buyers. | After first 30 orders |
| 4\. Create brand preference | Only possible after stage 3\. Requires accumulated positive experience. | Accumulated reviews. UGC advocacy loop. Waitlist growth. DCRM asset development. | After first 100 orders |
| 5\. Generate brand loyalty | Long-term. Not the launch objective. | Repeat buyer programme. Limited artist drops for returning buyers. Email / WhatsApp CRM. | 6+ months post-launch |

*Source: Wirtz, Digital Business and Electronic Commerce, §17.5 — Digital Brand Development Process (2024)*

**Implication for all decisions at launch:** Every product page element, every caption, every UI choice is assessed against one question: does this help a first-time visitor trust HORO enough to place one order? If not, it is not a launch priority.

## **1.2  Waitlist — The First CRM Asset**

NEW IN V1.9 — Wirtz §17.6 (DCRM — Digital Customer Relationship Management): for a new brand with no purchase history, the first CRM action is building the notification list. A waitlist built at drop 1 is worth more than any paid ad campaign at drop 2\.

| What to collect | Why it matters |
| :---- | :---- |
| WhatsApp number (primary) or email (secondary). Collected via the 'Be first when the next drop opens' section on the website and story link in bio. | The waitlist converts warm interest into a notification right (they asked to hear from you). First-party data. No algorithm dependency. At launch, one drop announcement to a list of 200 people who asked is more valuable than 10,000 ad impressions to cold audiences. |
| What to send: one message, one time, when the drop goes live. No newsletters. No promotions. | Respecting the permission maintains list quality. Overuse collapses it. The waitlist only works if members trust HORO will not spam them. |

# **2\.  Curation Logic — The Visible Eye**

The artist is credited as 'Illustrated by \[Name\]' — one quiet line below the title, in Bougainvillea. This is the art-hero, artist-attribute architecture.

| Filter | The question HORO asks | Why it matters to the buyer | V1.9 content rule |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| 1\. Does the work speak on its own? | If you need to explain the illustration before someone reacts to it, it does not belong on fabric. Two seconds. | Aesthetic self-expression buyer: 'This looks like me.' Needs uniqueness proof without explanation. | **Illustration title leads.** Arabic \+ English. Cairo Bold. Cobalt Nile. This is the first text element on every product card and post. |
| 2\. Is there a real person behind it? | A name, a studio, a reason they made it. No anonymous work. | Support-local-artist buyer: 'I wore someone's real work.' Triggered by attribution and behind-the-scenes content. | **Attribution, not feature.** 'Illustrated by \[Name\]' — one line, below title, Bougainvillea, small. Artist is not the headline. HORO is the curator. The illustration is the subject. |
| 3\. Will it survive being owned? | Three washes minimum. Real light. On a real body. If the illustration does not hold up, it does not go out. | Portrait-aesthetic buyer: 'It looks like an art print, not a sticker.' Needs macro print proof. | **Proof before claim.** Weight photo, wash-test before/after, macro ink detail. Visible on every product page. Sourced to Wirtz §17.5 and Oetzel Ch.3. |
| 4\. Can someone give this with pride? | Not pride in spending — pride in understanding the recipient. If the gift does not say 'I know you,' it fails. | Gift buyer: 'I gave something with meaning.' Triggered by occasions. Needs delivery guarantee \+ premium unboxing. | **Gift frame always available.** Price anchored against gold (2,000+ EGP) and perfume (800+ EGP), not against commodity tees. # **3\.  Purchasing Psychology Architecture**

NEW IN V1.9 — 12 phenomena from Oetzel & Luppold (33 Phenomena of Purchasing Decisions, Springer 2024\) applied to HORO's product pages, caption writing, and pricing architecture. These are not optional enhancements — each one addresses a specific conversion barrier identified in the Egypt market research.

| Chapter | Phenomenon | How HORO applies it | Where it appears |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Ch.17 | Left-Digit Effect | Price displays as **699 EGP** with strikethrough 899 EGP. The reference price is mandatory — not optional. Without the anchor, 699 EGP reads as 'expensive.' With it, it reads as 'value.' | Hero section. Product card. Cart total. |
| Ch.29 | Scarcity — Quantity, Not Time | **'3 remaining'** or **'7 pieces — founding batch'**. Quantity-based scarcity is credible. Time-based countdowns ('order in the next 2 hours') trigger persuasion-knowledge skepticism online. Never use countdown timers. | Product card. Hero section. Cart page. |
| Ch.5 | Compromise Effect | Three pricing tiers: 599 (Core) / **699 — MOST CHOSEN** / 899 (Limited). The middle tier is highlighted. Buyers anchor to the middle option. This is not a promotion — it is choice architecture. | Collection grid. Tier selector bar. |
| Ch.6 | Confirmation Bias | Aggregate star rating (**★★★★★ 4.9**) shown FIRST — before individual reviews. Buyers then seek information confirming the initial positive signal. Aggregate shown above illustration title (Wasilewski RSTT modification). | Product card. Hero section. |
| Ch.27 | Reciprocity | **'0 EGP delivery'** — not 'free delivery.' The phrasing '0 EGP' makes the concrete value of the gift explicit and triggers reciprocity. 'Free' is expected; '0 EGP on orders over 700' is a specific gesture. | Nav bar. Trust badges. Free delivery bar. |
| Ch.1 | Absolute Number Effect | Gift price comparison states: HORO 699 EGP vs perfume 800+ EGP vs gold 2,000+ EGP. The absolute gap (1,301 EGP between HORO and gold) reads as larger than a percentage. Specific numbers, never percentages. | Gifting section. Price objection DM template. |
| Ch.11 | Handmade Effect | Consumers pay a premium for handmade/process-visible work — but only when the process is actually shown. 'Made with love' without proof has zero effect on WTP. Mandatory: weight scale photo, wash-test photo, macro ink detail. | Product page proof section. Drop content. |
| Ch.3 | Asymmetric Information | Buyer cannot verify quality before purchase (no physical inspection). Credible quality signals substitute for direct inspection. The four mandatory proof shots are the mechanism. | All product pages. Trust badges section. |
| Ch.31 | Silver-Lining Effect | Scarcity signal ('3 remaining') and price anchor ('strikethrough 899') appear in separate visual zones — never combined in the same sentence. Combining them creates message conflict and reduces both effects. | Product card layout rule. |
| Ch.12 | Impulse Buying | Secondary product placement appears in the gifting section — after the main product CTA, not before. This is the correct sequence: primary decision first, impulse option visible after commitment signal. | Gifting section. Cart page. |
| Ch.9 | Social Norms Descriptive | **Only apply when data exists.** 'المختار من 147 شخص في القاهرة' (Chosen by 147 in Cairo) works only when the number is real. During launch (no data): remove. When 147+ orders exist: add. Never fabricate social norms signals. | Product card — deferred until data exists. |

*Source: Oetzel & Luppold, 33 Phenomena of Purchasing Decisions, Springer 2024\.*

# **4\.  Fashion Communication & Social Media Architecture**

V1.9 update: The palimpsest framework and drop narrative arc from V1.8 are retained. V1.9 adds the AIDASLA model (Sutherland) mapped to the drop cycle, the Jab×3 content principle, Freytag's Pyramid for illustration story posts, and the UGC advocacy loop.

## **4.1  AIDASLA Model — Drop Cycle Architecture**

Every drop cycle is mapped to Sutherland's AIDASLA model. Each phase has a single communication objective.

| Phase | AIDASLA Stage | What HORO does | Output format |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Days 1–2 (pre-drop) | A — Attention | Illustration reveal: the artwork, no product yet. Arabic-first caption. Strong visual. No price mentioned yet. | Instagram post. Story. Title overlay in Cobalt Nile. |
| Day 3 (pre-drop) | I — Interest | The story behind the illustration. Meaning-first copy (Freytag Act 1: exposition). Why HORO selected this piece. | Caption \+ reel. 'This is why we chose this.' |
| Day 4 (drop day) | D — Desire | Full product launch: 4-shot system. Model with measurements. GSM \+ print proof. Wash test. Price \+ reference price. | 6-shot product post. Story with COD \+ WhatsApp CTA. |
| Day 4 (drop day) | A — Action | Primary CTA: 'Order via WhatsApp' \+ 'Order online.' COD prominent. Delivery time stated. Quantity scarcity visible. | Story swipe-up. Bio link. WhatsApp pinned message. |
| Days 5–6 | S — Satisfaction | Post-purchase follow-up. WhatsApp check-in within 24hrs of delivery. UGC request with permission. | WhatsApp template. Story reshare of customer photos. |
| Day 6–7 | L — Loyalty | Archive the artist story. Cross-reference in future drops. Waitlist tease for Drop N+1. | Story highlight update. Waitlist signup prompt. |
| Ongoing | A — Advocacy | UGC reposts (with credit). Customer photo reshare. Prize incentive for photo uploads. | Feed repost. Story reshare. Caption credits customer \+ illustration title. |

*Source: Sutherland, Strategic Social Media Management, 2nd ed.*

## **4.2  Jab×3 Rule — Content Balance**

For every direct sales CTA post ('Order now'), HORO publishes three value posts: illustration story / behind-the-scenes / meaning-first content. Sutherland: 'Stop selling, start helping.' Buyers who receive three pieces of value before the ask are 3× more likely to respond to the CTA.

| Jab 1 | Jab 2 | Right Hook |
| :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Illustration story: What the artwork means. Why it was created. What emotion it carries. No purchase prompt. Arabic-first. | Process proof: Behind-the-scenes sketch, fabric detail, wash-test. Shows the HOW, not the WHAT. 'This is how a HORO piece is verified before it ships.' | Product CTA: Full 4-shot system. Price \+ reference price. Scarcity. WhatsApp \+ website CTA. COD prominent. Model measurements. |

## **4.3  Illustration Story Post — Freytag's Pyramid**

Every Drop N+1 illustration story post follows Freytag's 5-act structure. This format is used for the Day 3 (Interest) content in the AIDASLA arc.

| Act | Content for HORO illustration story |
| :---- | :---- |
| 1\. Exposition | Introduce the illustration and the artist's world. 'Cairo, 2025\. A studio in Dokki. An artist who has been drawing \[X\] for three years.' |
| 2\. Rising Action | The problem the illustration addresses, or the emotion it captures. 'The space between feeling something and being able to name it. Most art about this is either too loud or too vague.' |
| 3\. Climax | The illustration itself — the single frame that says it all in two seconds. |
| 4\. Falling Action | The curation moment. 'We looked at dozens of pieces. This one was the one that stayed.' |
| 5\. Resolution | The invitation. 'If it says something about you — it belongs on you.' \+ 'Illustrated by \[Name\].' \+ Drop date / order method. |

*Source: Sutherland, Strategic Social Media Management, 2nd ed. Freytag's Pyramid applied to fashion content.*

## **7.2  Homepage & Product Page UI Rules (Wasilewski)**

All modifications below are sourced to Wasilewski (2024) live testing on 607,570 sessions across 7 months. These are not design preferences — they are validated behavioural findings.

| Modification | Code | Rule | Measured effect |
| :---- | :---- | :---- | :---- |
| Stars above product title | RSTT | Aggregate star rating (★★★★★ 4.9) appears ABOVE the illustration title on all product cards and in the hero fold. Not below. Not alongside. Above. | Study 1: 41% CR improvement over standard interface. |
| Free delivery threshold progress bar | MITLC | Progress bar visible at top of page showing cart value vs. 700 EGP free delivery threshold. Background shifts to Felucca Teal when threshold is reached. | Proven microconversion trigger. Prompts add-to-cart behaviour to unlock the delivery reward. |
| Model info inside product card | MIDG | Height / weight / size worn visible inside the product card itself — not in a sizing tab, not on a separate page. | Reduces return rate risk. Buyer has information to make size decision without leaving the product card. |
| Size chart below sizes | LTSD | Link to full size chart placed BELOW the size selector, not above it. Matches decision sequence: choose size first, confirm with chart if unsure. | Tested against above-placement. Below placement outperformed. |
| Shortened delivery copy | DIST | Delivery information is short: **'COD · pay on delivery · Cairo 1-day · Nationwide 2-3 days'**. No redundant phrases. No 'product available' or 'estimated delivery subject to conditions.' | Shorter copy proved more effective than paragraph descriptions. |
| New visitor orientation strip | V1.9 | 3-step how-to-order strip (Choose → Order via WhatsApp or website → Delivered COD) visible immediately below navigation. Present for every visitor during launch phase — 100% of HORO's visitors are new. | Study 4: dedicated new-visitor interface \+13% CR and \+26% AOV vs. standard. Remove only when returning customer data exists. |

*Source: Wasilewski, Multi-variant User Interfaces in E-commerce, Appendix B and Chapter 5.3 (Springer 2024).*

**What Wasilewski does NOT add at launch:** AI clustering, ML segmentation, self-adaptive UI. These require a minimum of \~65,000 sessions of behavioural data. HORO has zero sessions at launch. These are post-launch tools.

# **9\.  Seasonal & Gifting Voice**

Gold-substitution frame: inflation shifted gifting from gold to premium clothing. HORO sits in this redirected spend. Oetzel Ch.1 (Absolute Number Effect): always frame price against the absolute gap, not percentages.

# **10\.  Service & Operations**

| Protocol | Standard |
| :---- | :---- |
| COD protocol | Pre-dispatch WhatsApp confirmation. Courier tracking sent. Post-delivery check-in within 24 hrs. RTO rate monitored weekly. |
| Exchange & returns | Arabic-language policy published (CPA 2023 compliant). Photo request is fair. Resolution within 24 hrs. Follow-up after resolution. 7-day exchange window stated on every product page and in every DM order confirmation. |
| DM response standard | Human tone. Never defensive. Price objection formula: **فاهمين** → Proof → Reframe. Sizing inquiry (Wasilewski MIDG): respond with exact model measurements and garment measurements. Always offer the WhatsApp sizing consultation before the buyer decides. |
| Artist collaboration | Transparent licensing. Variable royalty or revenue share. Credited work on all public content. On-time payouts. Brief includes: brand fit criteria (Section 2), illustration title hierarchy rule (Section 2), content guidelines, no 'print anything' language. |
| Waitlist management (Wirtz DCRM) | One message per drop — sent only when the drop is live. No promotional messages, no newsletters, no surveys. List quality degrades with overuse. Respect the permission or the asset collapses. |
| Crisis response | Own it immediately. Photo request is fair. Resolve within 24 hrs. Follow up after resolution. If public: factual, fair, brief. Never defensive in public. |

# **11\.  What HORO Must Never Become**

| ✘ Prohibition | Why (source) |
| :---- | :---- |
| A cheap print shop with nicer designs. | Egypt MR: 'print anything' operators exist at EGP 80–200. HORO's only defensible moat is curation and quality proof. Lose either and the business case collapses. |
| A vague 'art brand' with no physical proof. | Oetzel Ch.3 (Asymmetric Information) \+ Wirtz §17.5: without visible process proof, quality claims are unverifiable and have zero effect on WTP. |
| A tourist-style Egyptian heritage brand (pharaohs, hieroglyphs). | Karamalak (FACTUM 25): specific cultural aesthetics succeed; generic national pride does not. |
| A cold premium label with no warmth or service. | Five Core Values, V1.7. Service is the brand. |
| A pretty Instagram page that fails at delivery or COD. | Egypt MR: 60% COD dependency. A failed delivery is a brand-ending event at launch. |
| A brand that claims artist collaboration but hides the terms. | FACTUM 25 (Checa et al.): perceived fit between brand and artist requires visible, authentic relationship. |
| A brand with a voice but no origin story — personality without a past. (Added V1.8) | Zanola et al. (FACTUM 25): without a foundational narrative, brand claims have no authority. |
| A brand that says 'curated' but cannot show the eye behind the curation. (Added V1.8) | Curation logic must be public. Section 2 of this document makes it visible. |
| A brand that makes the artist the hero instead of the illustration. (Added V1.9) | HORO is the curator. The illustration is the subject. The artist is the credited creator. This hierarchy is non-negotiable. |
| A brand that uses countdown timers or fabricated social norms. (Added V1.9) | Oetzel Ch.29: time-based scarcity online triggers persuasion-knowledge skepticism. Oetzel Ch.9: social norms only apply when the number is real. |
| A brand that ignores the startup phase and skips to brand loyalty tactics. (Added V1.9) | Wirtz §17.5: the 5-stage brand development sequence cannot be skipped. Stage 1 (brand interest) and Stage 2 (first purchase) must be completed before loyalty work begins. |
